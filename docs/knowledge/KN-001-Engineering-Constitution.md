# Purpose

The Engineering Constitution defines the enduring engineering philosophy of ForgeLabs.

It serves as the highest-level engineering document within the organization and provides the principles that guide architectural decisions, engineering standards, product development, automation, and the use of artificial intelligence.

All engineering standards, specifications, processes, and implementations should align with this constitution.

---

# Article I — Build Assets, Not Features

ForgeLabs prioritizes creating reusable assets over isolated features.

Every meaningful piece of work should either:

* Create direct customer value, or
* Increase the capability, speed, quality, or scalability of future work.

Assets compound in value over time.

---

# Article II — Knowledge Is a First-Class Product

Knowledge is treated as a deliverable.

Architectural decisions, engineering standards, lessons learned, and technical rationale are preserved alongside source code.

Documentation exists to transfer understanding, not simply to describe implementation.

---

# Article III — Architecture Before Implementation

Important implementation work begins with understanding the problem.

Engineering decisions should be guided by architecture, not by convenience or the capabilities of a specific tool.

Well-designed systems reduce long-term complexity.

---

# Article IV — Simplicity Is a Competitive Advantage

The simplest solution that satisfies current requirements is preferred.

Complexity must be justified.

Abstractions should emerge from real implementation experience rather than speculation.

---

# Article V — Automate Repetitive Work

Engineers should spend their time solving problems, not repeating manual processes.

Any repetitive activity that provides long-term value should be considered a candidate for automation.

Automation is an investment in future productivity.

---

# Article VI — Quality Is Built In

Quality is achieved through engineering discipline rather than inspection alone.

Every contribution should leave the codebase, documentation, or engineering process in a better state than it was found.

Broken builds, avoidable technical debt, and undocumented architectural decisions should not become the normal state of the system.

---

# Article VII — Engineering Decisions Must Be Traceable

Important technical decisions should be documented with sufficient context and rationale.

Future engineers should be able to understand not only what decision was made, but why it was made.

Knowledge that exists only in conversations is considered incomplete.

---

# Article VIII — AI Augments Engineering Judgment

Artificial intelligence is an engineering collaborator.

AI assists with implementation, analysis, documentation, testing, and review.

Responsibility for architecture, engineering judgment, product direction, and final approval always remains with human engineers.

---

# Article IX — Continuous Improvement Over Perfection

ForgeLabs values continuous improvement through small, incremental progress.

Every milestone should produce a working, reviewable improvement.

Learning from implementation is preferred over excessive upfront speculation.

---

# Article X — Build for the Long Term

Engineering decisions should consider long-term maintainability, adaptability, and clarity.

Technologies, frameworks, and tools will change.

Engineering principles, disciplined thinking, and well-preserved knowledge create lasting value.

ForgeLabs builds software intended to improve with time rather than accumulate unnecessary complexity.

---

# Living Principle

This constitution is intentionally stable.

Changes should be rare, deliberate, and made only when they improve the long-term engineering philosophy of ForgeLabs rather than addressing short-term implementation concerns.

All future engineering standards, architectural decisions, specifications, and software should align with these principles.
